Obsidian Bank Restoration

A meticulous restoration of a 1920s financial institution

Location

Downtown Historic District

Year Completed

2022

Project Duration

18 months

Building Type

Commercial / Financial

Project Overview

The Obsidian Bank building, constructed in 1925, stands as a testament to the Neoclassical architectural style that dominated financial institutions of the early 20th century. Our comprehensive restoration project focused on preserving the building's historic character while adapting it for modern use.

Restoration Scope

  • Facade restoration and stone cleaning
  • Ornamental metalwork repair and replication
  • Historic window rehabilitation
  • Interior plasterwork restoration
  • Period-appropriate lighting fixture restoration
  • Modern systems integration

Restoration Process

01

Assessment & Documentation

Comprehensive architectural survey and condition assessment of all historic elements

02

Material Conservation

Careful cleaning and restoration of original limestone facade and decorative elements

03

Structural Reinforcement

Strategic upgrades to ensure building stability while preserving historic fabric

04

Interior Restoration

Restoration of original banking hall, including marble floors and coffered ceiling

Restoration Gallery

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge

Deteriorated limestone facade with significant water damage and spalling

Solution

Implemented targeted stone restoration techniques and installed discrete drainage improvements

Challenge

Integration of modern systems while preserving historic interiors

Solution

Utilized existing chases and created discrete pathways for new mechanical systems

Sustainability Features

  • Energy-efficient window restoration maintaining historic profiles
  • Smart climate control systems integrated with historic radiators
  • LED lighting retrofits in restored historic fixtures
  • Low-VOC materials used throughout restoration
